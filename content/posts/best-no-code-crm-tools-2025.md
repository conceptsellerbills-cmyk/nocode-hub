# Best No-Code CRM Tools in 2025: Manage Customers Without Coding

A good CRM is the backbone of any customer-facing business — tracking contacts, deals, and conversations in one place. The best no-code CRM tools give small teams enterprise-grade relationship management without a developer or a six-figure software budget. Monthly search volume: ~30,000/month.

## What to Look for in a No-Code CRM

- **Contact management:** Store, tag, and search all contacts
- **Pipeline views:** Visual deal stages from lead to close
- **Automation:** Follow-ups, reminders, and status updates
- **Integrations:** Email, calendar, and marketing tools
- **Reporting:** Revenue forecasting and activity tracking

---

## Top 7 No-Code CRM Tools for 2025

### 1. HubSpot CRM — Best Free CRM

**Best for:** Startups and SMBs wanting a powerful, genuinely free CRM

HubSpot's CRM is free forever for unlimited users and contacts. Its contact timeline, deal pipeline, email tracking, and meeting scheduler are all free — making it the best starting point for any business.

**Strengths:**
- Free forever — unlimited users and contacts
- Deal pipeline with drag-and-drop stages
- Email tracking and templates
- Meeting scheduler linked to Google/Outlook calendar
- Activity timeline for every contact
- 1,000+ integrations

**Limitations:**
- Advanced automation requires paid Marketing Hub
- Reporting limited on free plan
- Interface can feel complex initially

**Pricing:** Free CRM; Starter $15/user/month; Professional $90/user/month

---

### 2. Pipedrive — Best for Sales Pipeline Management

**Best for:** Sales teams that want the most intuitive visual pipeline experience

Pipedrive is built by salespeople for salespeople. Its visual pipeline keeps deals organized, automated reminders prevent leads from going cold, and AI-powered insights surface which deals to prioritize.

**Strengths:**
- Best-in-class visual sales pipeline
- Activity-based selling methodology built-in
- AI Sales Assistant for deal insights
- Email sync and tracking
- Customizable pipeline stages and fields

**Limitations:**
- No built-in marketing tools
- Reporting limited on lower tiers
- Expensive for large teams

**Pricing:** Essential $14/user/month; Advanced $29/user/month; Professional $59/user/month

---

### 3. Notion as CRM — Best Custom No-Code CRM

**Best for:** Teams already using Notion who want a free, flexible CRM without a new tool

Notion's database and relation features let you build a fully custom CRM tailored to your exact process. Use free community templates or build your own with contacts, companies, deals, and tasks all linked.

**Strengths:**
- Completely free for most teams
- Customizable to any workflow
- Linked databases (contacts ↔ deals ↔ tasks)
- Multiple views: table, board, calendar, gallery
- Excellent team collaboration

**Limitations:**
- No email tracking or calling features
- Requires setup (not plug-and-play)
- No native automation (needs Zapier/Make)

**Pricing:** Free plan; Plus $10/month; Business $15/user/month

---

### 4. Airtable CRM — Best Visual Database CRM

**Best for:** Operations teams who want a CRM built on a flexible, visual database

Airtable's grid, kanban, and gallery views transform a database into a fully functional CRM. Its formula fields, automations, and interface designer let you build exactly the CRM your team needs.

**Strengths:**
- Flexible database with 30+ field types
- Kanban pipeline view built-in
- Interface Designer for custom apps
- Automations without Zapier
- Gallery view for visual contact browsing

**Limitations:**
- No native email sending
- Free plan limited to 1,000 records per base
- Learning curve for database thinking

**Pricing:** Free (1,000 records); Plus $10/user/month; Pro $20/user/month

---

### 5. Streak — Best CRM Inside Gmail

**Best for:** Gmail power users who want a CRM without leaving their inbox

Streak lives entirely inside Gmail — pipelines, contacts, email tracking, and snippets are all accessible from the same inbox you already use. Zero context-switching for email-driven sales processes.

**Strengths:**
- 100% inside Gmail — zero new tab
- Email tracking (open/click notifications)
- Mail merge for personalized outreach
- Pipeline views inside Gmail
- Free plan for individual use

**Limitations:**
- Gmail only — no other email clients
- Less powerful than dedicated CRMs
- Mobile app less capable than web

**Pricing:** Free (personal use); Solo $15/month; Pro $49/user/month

---

### 6. Folk — Best Modern CRM for Relationship-Driven Sales

**Best for:** Founders, investors, and consultants who prioritize relationship quality over volume

Folk is a modern CRM built for high-touch relationship management. Its magic fields auto-enrich contacts from LinkedIn, its grouping system manages different contact types, and its pipeline is simple and flexible.

**Strengths:**
- Magic fields: AI auto-enrichment from LinkedIn
- Clean, modern interface
- CSV import and LinkedIn extension
- Email sequences built-in
- Flexible groups for contacts

**Limitations:**
- Newer — smaller ecosystem
- Less automation than HubSpot
- No phone/calling features

**Pricing:** Standard $20/user/month; Premium $40/user/month

---

### 7. Zoho CRM — Best Value for Growing Teams

**Best for:** SMBs wanting a feature-rich CRM at a lower price than Salesforce

Zoho CRM offers advanced automation, AI predictions, multi-channel communication, and enterprise features at small business prices. Its breadth is unmatched at the price point.

**Strengths:**
- AI assistant (Zia) for sales predictions
- Multi-channel: email, phone, social, live chat
- Advanced workflow automation
- Territory management
- Extensive customization

**Limitations:**
- UI feels dated compared to modern tools
- Support quality varies
- Features can overwhelm smaller teams

**Pricing:** Free (3 users); Standard $14/user/month; Professional $23/user/month; Enterprise $40/user/month

## FAQ

### What is the best free no-code CRM?

HubSpot CRM is the best free CRM — unlimited contacts and users, with deal pipeline, email tracking, and meeting scheduling all free. For a custom option, Notion or Airtable CRM templates are excellent free alternatives.

### Can Notion replace a CRM?

For relationship-driven businesses with smaller contact volumes (under 500 contacts), Notion can effectively replace a dedicated CRM. For sales teams needing email tracking, sequences, and automation, a purpose-built CRM like HubSpot is more efficient.

### What CRM do startups use?

Most early-stage startups use HubSpot (free), Notion (custom), or Pipedrive (sales-focused). As they scale, many migrate to Salesforce or HubSpot Professional for more automation and reporting.
