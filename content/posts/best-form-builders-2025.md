Online forms are the backbone of business data collection — from lead capture and customer surveys to job applications and payment collection. The best form builders in 2025 go far beyond basic fields: they include conditional logic, payment processing, file uploads, multi-step flows, and integrations with every major business tool. With over 500,000 monthly searches, choosing the right form builder is a practical business decision that affects conversion rates, data quality, and workflow efficiency.

## What Makes a Great Form Builder?

**Ease of use**: Drag-and-drop builders that don't require coding. Anyone on your team should be able to create a form.

**Conditional logic**: Show or hide fields based on previous answers. Essential for surveys, applications, and complex intake forms.

**Integrations**: Connect to your CRM, email marketing tool, Slack, Google Sheets, Zapier, etc.

**Customization**: Match your brand colors, fonts, and style. Generic-looking forms reduce trust and conversion.

**Multi-step/conversational forms**: Long forms broken into steps (or conversational Q&A format) significantly improve completion rates.

**Analytics and reporting**: Response rates, average completion time, drop-off points.

**Security and compliance**: GDPR compliance, encrypted submissions, HIPAA options for healthcare.

## Best Form Builders 2025

### 1. Typeform
**Price: Free (10 responses/month); Basic $29/month; Plus $59/month**

Typeform pioneered the conversational form format — presenting one question at a time in a chat-like interface. This approach dramatically increases completion rates (Typeform reports 2x higher completion rates than traditional forms for many use cases).

**Standout features:**
- Beautiful, conversational one-question-at-a-time format
- Logic jumps and question branching
- 120+ templates across industries
- Rich media integration (images, GIFs in questions)
- AI form generation (describe your form, AI creates it)
- Integrations: HubSpot, Salesforce, Mailchimp, Zapier (2,000+ apps)
- Calculators and quizzes with score display

**Best for**: Lead generation forms, surveys, quizzes, application forms. Any situation where user experience matters and completion rate is critical.

**Limitation**: Expensive at higher response volumes. Free plan is very limited.

### 2. Tally
**Price: Free (unlimited responses); Pro $29/month**

Tally is the best free form builder available. Its Notion-like interface lets you build forms using familiar text editing — just type "/" to add any form element. Unlimited responses on the free plan with no Tally branding.

**Standout features:**
- Free unlimited responses (no monthly limits)
- No Tally branding on free plan
- Notion-inspired editor (very fast to use)
- Conditional logic included free
- File uploads, signatures, payment collection
- Real-time response notifications
- Hidden fields for tracking UTM parameters

**Best for**: Startups, freelancers, and any business that wants powerful forms without a subscription. Tally's free plan beats most competitors' paid plans.

### 3. JotForm
**Price: Free (5 forms, 100 responses/month); Bronze $34/month; Silver $39/month**

JotForm is one of the most feature-rich form builders available, with 10,000+ templates and deep customization options. Particularly strong for complex forms with payment processing.

**Standout features:**
- 10,000+ form templates
- Payment processing (Stripe, PayPal, Square, 30+ gateways)
- E-signatures (JotForm Sign)
- PDF generation from submissions
- HIPAA compliance available (Enterprise)
- Approvals workflow (multi-step review and approval)
- 150+ app integrations

**Best for**: Healthcare and professional services needing HIPAA compliance, businesses needing payment collection in forms, teams needing approval workflows.

### 4. Google Forms
**Price: Free**

Google Forms is completely free, integrates seamlessly with Google Sheets, and is simple to use. The forms are functional and clean, though not as visually sophisticated as Typeform or Tally.

**Best for**: Internal surveys, school/education use, simple data collection where aesthetics are less important. Google Workspace users who want zero cost.

**Limitations**: No conditional logic branching to skip questions based on answers (limited compared to dedicated tools), basic design options, no payment collection.

### 5. SurveyMonkey
**Price: Free (10 questions, 100 responses); Team plans from $25/user/month**

SurveyMonkey is the most recognized survey platform globally, with robust analytics, benchmarking data, and specialized survey templates.

**Standout features:**
- Industry benchmark data (compare your results to industry averages)
- Advanced analytics and cross-tabulation
- Panel surveys (pay to send surveys to specific audience segments)
- NPS, CSAT, and CES question types built in
- AI question suggestions

**Best for**: Market research, customer satisfaction surveys, employee engagement surveys — especially when analytics and benchmarking matter.

**Limitation**: Expensive for the feature set; Typeform and Tally offer better UX at lower (or zero) cost for most use cases.

### 6. Paperform
**Price: From $24/month (Essentials)**

Paperform takes a document-like approach to forms — combining form fields with rich text, images, and video in a page-like format. This makes it excellent for form/landing page hybrids.

**Standout features:**
- Document-style builder (feels like creating a web page, not just a form)
- Payment collection (Stripe, PayPal, Braintree)
- Appointment scheduling integration
- Calculations and formulas in forms
- Product catalogs (for simple order forms)

**Best for**: Event registrations, job applications, order forms that need more context and explanation around the fields.

### 7. Formstack
**Price: From $83/month**

Enterprise-grade form builder with strong workflow automation, compliance features, and Salesforce integration.

**Best for**: Large organizations with compliance requirements, Salesforce-heavy sales teams, complex approval workflows.

## Tips for Higher Form Completion Rates

**Keep it short**: Every additional question reduces completion rate. Remove every field that isn't absolutely necessary.

**Use multi-step forms**: Breaking long forms into steps shows progress and reduces overwhelm. Completion rates for multi-step forms are typically 2–3x higher than single-page forms.

**Explain why you're asking**: For sensitive fields (phone number, company revenue), briefly explain why you need the information.

**Use conversational language**: Write questions as if you're talking to the person, not filling out a form. "What's your biggest challenge?" vs. "Challenges: (select all that apply)"

**Remove unnecessary friction**: Pre-fill fields where possible (from URL parameters), use address autocomplete, enable autofill.

**Test on mobile**: 70%+ of form submissions may come from mobile devices. A form that's annoying to fill out on mobile loses those conversions.

## Conclusion

For most businesses and freelancers, **Tally** is the best starting point — unlimited free responses with conditional logic and excellent integrations. For higher-converting lead gen forms where aesthetics matter, **Typeform** is worth the investment. For payment collection and complex workflows, **JotForm** has the broadest feature set.

The right form builder depends less on which is "best overall" and more on your specific use case, budget, and integration needs.
