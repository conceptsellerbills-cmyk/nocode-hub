# Best No-Code Membership Site Builders in 2025

Membership sites and paid communities are one of the most predictable revenue streams available — monthly recurring income from subscribers who pay for exclusive content, tools, or community access. No-code platforms make launching one easier than ever. Monthly search volume: ~18,000/month.

## Types of No-Code Membership Platforms

- **Community-first:** Circle, Mighty Networks, Skool
- **Content-gating tools:** Memberstack, Memberspace
- **Course + membership:** Teachable, Kajabi
- **Newsletter subscriptions:** Beehiiv, Substack

---

## Top 7 No-Code Membership Site Builders for 2025

### 1. Circle — Best Community Platform for Creators

**Best for:** Creators and educators wanting a beautiful, engagement-focused community platform

Circle is the premier community platform for professional creators — used by Ali Abdaal, Sahil Bloom, and thousands of course creators. Its Spaces (discussion, courses, events, chat) create a structured community experience far superior to Discord or Facebook Groups.

**Strengths:**
- Beautiful, modern community interface
- Spaces: discussions, courses, events, live streams, chats
- Courses with video hosting included
- Paid membership and free tiers
- Gamification and member profiles
- Zapier and API integrations
- White-label custom domain

**Limitations:**
- Expensive compared to Skool
- No free plan
- Less content-focused than Kajabi

**Pricing:** Basic $89/month; Professional $199/month; Business $360/month; Enterprise custom

---

### 2. Skool — Best for Course + Community Bundles

**Best for:** Online educators wanting a simple, gamified community tied to their courses

Skool combines community and courses in a clean, gamified platform. Its points and leaderboard system drives engagement, and its simple classroom keeps course content organized. Alex Hormozi's investment brought massive growth in 2024.

**Strengths:**
- Dead-simple interface — best UX of any platform
- Community + courses in one place
- Gamification (points, leaderboard, badges)
- Calendars for events and calls
- Flat pricing — no revenue percentage
- Growing ecosystem with peer referrals

**Limitations:**
- Limited customization
- No email marketing built-in
- No advanced analytics

**Pricing:** $99/month flat (unlimited members and courses)

---

### 3. Memberstack — Best for Gating Existing Websites

**Best for:** Businesses with existing websites (Webflow, WordPress, custom) who want to add membership features

Memberstack is a "membership layer" that adds payment, authentication, and content gating to any existing website. If you've already built your site in Webflow or elsewhere, Memberstack is the fastest way to add paid memberships.

**Strengths:**
- Works with any website (Webflow, HTML, React)
- Stripe-powered payments
- Content gating with CSS classes
- Multiple membership tiers
- Free and trial memberships
- Zapier integrations

**Limitations:**
- Not a full community platform
- Requires existing website
- No built-in content delivery

**Pricing:** Free (2% transaction fee); Basic $25/month (1%); Pro $49/month (0%)

---

### 4. Mighty Networks — Best for Large, Feature-Rich Communities

**Best for:** Organizations running large communities with live events, courses, and subgroups

Mighty Networks is a full-featured community platform with native live streaming, courses, events, subgroups (Spaces), and a mobile app — all under your custom brand. Ideal for associations, mastermind groups, and enterprise communities.

**Strengths:**
- Native iOS and Android apps with your branding
- Live streaming and video hosting
- Courses with quizzes and progress tracking
- Subgroups for segmented communities
- Member-to-member messaging
- Multiple payment models (subscription, one-time, bundles)

**Limitations:**
- Transaction fees on lower plans
- UI feels older than Circle or Skool
- Expensive at scale

**Pricing:** Community $41/month; Courses $99/month; Business $179/month; Mighty Pro (custom app) from $360/month

### 5. Kajabi — Best All-in-One Creator Platform

**Best for:** Serious creator businesses wanting courses, membership, email, website, and podcast in one

Kajabi is the all-in-one platform for creator businesses — it handles your website, blog, email marketing, online courses, membership, podcast, and coaching in one integrated platform. Higher price, but potentially replaces 5+ separate tools.

**Strengths:**
- All-in-one: website + email + courses + community + podcast
- Beautiful templates
- Built-in email marketing with automation
- No transaction fees
- Affiliate program built-in
- 24/7 support

**Limitations:**
- Expensive — starts at $149/month
- Community features less advanced than Circle
- Overkill for simple course/membership setups

**Pricing:** Kickstarter $69/month (25 customers); Basic $149/month; Growth $199/month; Pro $399/month

### 6. Patreon — Best for Creator Patronage Model

**Best for:** Artists, podcasters, and creators wanting a simple patronage subscription platform

Patreon is the original creator membership platform. Its discover feature gives access to Patreon's 8 million active patrons. Simple tier-based memberships with automatic monthly billing make it the easiest entry point.

**Strengths:**
- Built-in discover marketplace (8M+ patrons)
- Simple tier system
- Works for any content type (video, audio, writing, art)
- Free to start (percentage of revenue model)
- iOS and Android app

**Limitations:**
- Patreon takes 5-12% of revenue
- Limited customization
- Community features basic compared to Circle
- No courses or structured learning

**Pricing:** Free to launch; Lite 5%, Pro 8%, Premium 12% of revenue

---

### 7. Substack — Best for Paid Newsletter Communities

**Best for:** Writers and journalists building paid newsletter subscriptions with community discussion

Substack makes it simple to charge for newsletter subscriptions and add community threads. Its network effect and recommendation system give new publications an organic discovery channel unavailable on other platforms.

**Strengths:**
- Free to start — Substack takes 10% of paid revenue
- Built-in recommendation network (discovery)
- Comment threads for community discussion
- Podcast hosting included
- Chat feature for subscriber community
- iOS and Android apps

**Limitations:**
- Substack takes 10% forever
- Limited design customization
- Community less powerful than Circle
- Difficult to migrate subscribers to another platform

**Pricing:** Free to publish; 10% of paid subscriptions

## FAQ

### What is the best no-code membership platform?

Circle is the best for community-first memberships. Skool is the best for simplicity and gamification. Kajabi is best if you want everything in one platform and are willing to pay the premium.

### How much does it cost to build a membership site?

You can start for free on Patreon or Substack (they take a revenue percentage instead). Dedicated platforms start at $41-99/month. Budget $50-200/month for a professional membership site with your own domain.

### Can I use Webflow as a membership site?

Yes — with Memberstack added to your Webflow site, you can gate content, manage paid subscriptions, and create member-only areas. Webflow handles the design; Memberstack handles the membership logic.
